# Genre Profile: Strategy

Strategy games emphasize systems thinking, resource management, long-term planning,
and competitive depth. Sessions vary widely (5-60 min). Revenue is IAP-driven with
emphasis on acceleration, premium units, and competitive advantages. Economy design
and system interactions are the critical design pillars.

## Questioning Emphasis

- Chapter 3: What decisions does the player make? How do those decisions interact? What creates the strategic depth? What are the win/loss conditions?
- Chapter 4: Economy loops are the backbone -- how do resources flow? What creates meaningful trade-offs? How do expansion cycles work?
- Chapter 5: Map/world structure, campaign progression, PvP arena design -- how does content scale with player power?
- Chapter 6: Unit/building balance, economy tuning, PvP matchmaking fairness -- how do you prevent pay-to-win perception?
- Chapter 8: Alliance/guild obligations, territory control, seasonal ladder resets -- what creates long-term commitment?
- Chapter 10: Premium currency flow, speedup philosophy, competitive fairness -- how does monetization avoid destroying strategic integrity?
